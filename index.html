Why Trust Us

We are intentional about grooming leaders of tomorrow and producing the citizenry we envision today.
At Fundanomics we understand the importance and significance of investing in our youth through education.
We strive to tackle the illiteracy rates in South African youth.
We promote patriotism at a young age through teaching the basic tenets of constitutional democracy. Familiarizing the youth with our country, civil society, and the institutions we call democracy.
We understand how social transformation can come about through education - and how class disparities can and should be addressed through the pillar that is education.

Statistics on SA literacy

Basic literacy among children in South Africa has declined. A new report from the 2030 Reading Panel has found.
Most children leave grade one without knowing the alphabet, while 82% of grade 4 children cannot read for meaning.
Fewer primary school children can read for meaning now than before the COVID pandemic.
Meaning becomes familiar when the child can resonate with the environment. On a daily basis children are accustomed to certain jargon and lexicon based on familiarity.

Why we offer what we offer

As we transition towards a different epoch in South Africa,
it becomes vital to teach young people about society and
more importantly the legal pillars that protect and
strengthen democracy


We dare not fail the young because of the current epoch.
Children must understand the significance of the
democratic dispensation and its significance in shaping
society. Why people vote, why people petition, how people
must petition. What parliament and legislature mean.


The democracy pack is a series of books for children
between 7 and 12 to teach about the basics concepts of
South African democracy.

Sample books

The books are based on two young characters; Tammy and Themba
(Both aged 10). The two characters are part of a class taught
by the brilliant teacher, Miss Khumalo.

The individual books pertain fundamental themes within
democratic dispensation. The themes are:

Ubuntu

Democracy

The Constitution

The Economy

Patriotism
